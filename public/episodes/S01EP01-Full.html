The Stigma of Mental Health with Colette Barron

Posted by Liam Sawyer — Mental Health — 1 Comment
27Oct

Introduction

Liam: Welcome to Urban Garden Dreams, where we discuss whatever is on my mind. I’m Liam Sawyer, and with me is Colette Barron. Today, we’ll be talking about Mental Health with our special guest. Let’s get started!

L: So, this week's episode topic is very dear to me because it is something that I personally have struggled with and something I continue to struggle with. There is such a stigma behind taking care of your mental health and knowing when it’s time to listen to your mind and give it the attention it needs. We often go through life ignoring the small signs inside us but also the signs that other people give off, trying to show that they are not okay.

Colette: Come join us as we talk about why there is such a stigma and how we can help not only ourselves but for others that are struggling because if one thing is certain, we are not alone and our shared experiences only build us up and make us better.

Disclaimer

L: Just a disclaimer before we begin, we are not mental health professionals and the things discussed in this episode are from our own personal experiences and/or information gathered from NAMI, the National Alliance on Mental Illness. If you or a loved one is in need of someone to talk to, please call The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 800-273-TALK (8255) or call 911 immediately.

Mental Health

L: So why did I pick this as the first podcast episode? Well, as I mentioned earlier, it is something I have struggled with, and being a college student, I am surrounded by people and things that can make life a little harder than it needs to be. From weekend events to classes, there are triggers for everyone. College can be very hard especially for freshmen who have this new sense of freedom with the added pressure of maintaining their studies. On the other hand, it can be just as hard if not harder for the upperclassmen that are dealing with the added stress of trying to find internships and even job opportunities for when they graduate. Besides college life, there is the added stress of home, work, and even personal life. We will dive into these topics so we hope you stay with us.

C: One thing that we as college students do not do enough is taking mental health days to rejuvenate and recover from a busy and stressful week. We are so caught up in the party scene that we push ourselves to go out every weekend and drink to the point where we don’t remember what happened. This is caused by our fear of missing out or simply FOMO. We see people post on Snapchat and even Instagram a party and the first thought that comes to our mind is, “why am I not there.” This gets even worse if you were not asked to come so you just get inside your head and start to second guess everything. “Are they my friends, do they like me” and “why didn’t I get invited.”

C: This should not be the first thing to think about. Who cares what someone else is doing. Focus on yourself and try to be a better person, try to be a better version of who you want to become, not what others want you to be. Your goal is not to please everyone. Heck, that’s impossible. There will always be people that don’t like you and that is perfectly okay.

L: There are some weekends that I don’t have the motivation to go out because my social meter has run out. I will find the joy of staying inside and being with myself. From a simple pint of ice cream to catching up on my Netflix obsessions, I choose to do something that makes me happy. One thing that makes me really happy is listening to music. I go everywhere listening to music because it is so therapeutic. I enjoy the songs that have great lyrics with meanings that I can relate to. From indie-folk to pop, I generally like to listen to everything. My music obsession might be out of hand as Apple Music has said that I have listened to music this year alone for 709 hours with the album, The Weatherman by Gregory Alan Isakov on replay 455 times. Go check him out because his lyrics are absolutely legendary.

C: Sometimes I have a really hard time getting out of bed in the morning. Even if it's for something very important like class or an event. It's not because I don’t care or because I'm not prioritizing it, it is because I am anxious for the day even though I have no idea of what it is going to be like. Therefore I usually turn to call my family members. I love having a close relationship with them because it brings me a sense of comfort and happiness. When I'm feeling very down my sister and I will do city dates together which always makes me feel better. Activities that we do together usually consist of shopping, eating, or seeing different places in New York City. Personally, I also utilize shopping as a way to make me feel better because I know that I’ll have something new to look forward to.

Questions

For the next segment of the podcast, we will be answering some questions about mental health and related topics. The questions are below but are not transcribed. We want the listner to enjoy our answers as they were live and not planned before recording the episode.

  • What is your story about mental health?
  • Have you seen a therapist, for how long if not currently, and why did you stop?
  • What is the biggest challenge you face dealing with your mental health?
  • What is one thing you want others to know about mental health?
